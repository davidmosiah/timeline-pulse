# Timeline Pulse

An interactive atlas of timeline-shift folklore, Mandela Effect reports,
collider-era myths, and symbolic reality stories — every claim labeled, every
source graded.

Timeline Pulse is both a public website and an MCP server. It organizes
strange reports and recurring motifs into a searchable corpus for humans and
AI agents. A cultural archive, not a proof engine.

## Explore

- Archive: search 20 seed reports by motif, evidence grade, and text.
- Timeline: official CERN anchors and community story waves on one axis,
  never merged — including Long Shutdown 3 (2026-06-29 → ~2030).
- Motifs: a constellation of 17 recurring symbols (machine silence, rainbow
  bridge, serpent time, heart collider, timeline bleed…).
- Mandela Catalog: classic memory variants with community and mainstream
  explanations side by side.
- Story Threads: eight arcs, each written through belief, neutral, and
  skeptical lenses.

## MCP

```bash
npx -y timeline-pulse
```

Tools: `timeline_search_reports`, `timeline_get_report`,
`timeline_mandela_catalog`, `timeline_motif_map`, `timeline_story_threads`,
`timeline_events`, `timeline_compare_sources`, `timeline_deepen_story`,
`timeline_stats`, `timeline_agent_manifest`.

## Source stance

Official science events are separated from personal reports, community
interpretations, and speculative stories. Timeline Pulse does not claim that
CERN, the LHC, or particle physics proves timeline manipulation.
